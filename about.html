Self Evaluation

Personality

Patient individuals. I enjoy listening to others and are calm and rational with my responses. I do my best to avoid conflict, exhibiting a rather passive style when it comes to confrontation. Although I am not outwardly assertive, I am very understanding of personal and emotional issues.

Motivations

My personalities are motivated by stability and predictability, always seeking work that is void of interruptions or distractions from the task itself. Security and loyalty are also very important to me, in that I want to be certain my work conforms to the expectations of others. I value accuracy and precision but are fortunate enough to have the patience to meet these standards.

Communication

Speak in a calm and steady tone, ensuring you stay objective and minimize emotionally charged situations. Please ask lots of questions and explain your reasoning with details

Under Stress

When met with stress. My generally calm and rational approach to my work -- coupled with me by non-assertive style -- makes me appear detached, or potentially passive-aggressive.

Learning Style is Logical

I like using my brain for logical and mathematical reasoning. I can recognize patterns easily, as well as connections between seemingly meaningless content. This also leads me to classify and group information to help me learn or understand it.

I aim to understand the reasons behind the content and skills. Don't just rote learn. Understanding more detail behind the compulsory content helps me memorize and learn the material that I need to know. Explore the link between various systems, and note them down.

While I am studying, create and use lists by extracting key points from the material. I may also want to use statistics and other analysis to help myself identify areas may want to concentrate on.

Make use of 'systems thinking' to help understand the links between various parts of a system. An important point here is that systems thinking helps me understand the bigger picture. Often the whole is greater than the sum of the parts. For example, I may understand the individual aircraft systems and flight surfaces, but may not have a view of how all those systems support flight in equilibrium. Systems diagrams can help me gain that understanding.
